General Systems Theory

Rich Hildred - rhildred@wlu.ca - 519-594-0900

You wake up over the mideast ... over north Africa.

You go down, over the Indian Ocean ... and look up at the subcontinent of India.

And Up Across that monstrous Pacific Ocean ... you never realized how big it is.

And you finally come up across the coast of California, and look for those friendly things.

And down across New Orleans, and there's the peninsula of Florida.

And you go out across the Atlantic Ocean, and back across Africa.

That identity shifts, as you go around and around in an hour and a half ... you identify with the whole thing.

The earth is an indivisible whole, just as each of us is an indivisible whole. All boundaries ... are fundamentally arbitrary. We invent them and then, ironically, we find ourselves trapped within them.

Something made sales growth slow ... the need for the Wondertech Computer was still strong. Systems theory steps back and looks at the system as a whole. Find structural patterns in the details.
